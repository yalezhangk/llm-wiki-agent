## Summary
[[BLink]] is the named busbar-link technology emphasized in [[GHA]] documentation. It connects GHA panels while avoiding gas handling outside the manufacturer's site, which makes extension, disassembly, dismantling, and even moving a panel within a switchboard row simpler for a [[气体绝缘开关柜]] installation.

## Key Details
- B-link is described as the distinctive GHA feature behind the phrase "the switchgear with B-link".
- It enables on-site assembly and extension without gas handling outside the manufacturer's site.
- It allows a switchgear panel to be moved within the switchboard row without gas handling.
- The 2018 catalog describes B-link as IP65 and maintenance-free, like the gas-filled compartments.
- The busbar connection uses single-pole insulation with a robust silicone-rubber insulator.
- The arrangement is earthed and safe against contact voltage.
- Type-test statements in the deck include 40.5kV / BIL 185kV for the B-link arrangement.
- The older presentation states the screw-type contact was type-tested at 40kA for 3s and 104kA peak current.
- The 2018 catalog states the current-carrying B-link connections were checked at 40kA for 3s and 100kA peak current, creating a small version/documentation difference to verify before engineering use.
